Who am I? that very much depends on what you mean by the word 'am'. I find the verb 'to be' very odd. There is a deterministic, factual sense to this word.
take this last sentence for example; I find the verb to be very odd has a different sense compared to The verb 'to be' is an odd verb. This verb, I think, has a certain objective connotation that other words do not have. 
some people have decided to omit this verb from their language altogether, check out english-prime if this sounds interesting to you.

anyway, all that to say that who I am is a vague question to begin with. to answer it, I am going to presume some context to the word 'be'. 
when asked in a party from a person that doesn't know me, I would take this as a homo sepian sepaian asking another, what is the essence, the bare minimum, the core of what make me me. 
and eventhough I was born iran, and I am a male, and I weigh ~82kgs, and I am ~188 tall, I am not any of those. the verb 'to be' is not fine enough so let's use the word identify.
I do not identify as a heavy, tall, iranian dude I guess. another way I think about it is that you can change my gender, cut me short, weigh me up, and change my natioality, and I would still call me masani. 
then the question that arise is that, who am I then?

I guess I am a curious homo sapien sapien, an ape decendent of the anamalia genus type homo. I like creative endeavors, and that's it. 
but then ain't I an exact copy of many other homo sapien sapiens?


I thought I was the only one who felt like it, but turned out there are many others (look up englishPrime, or the institue for semantics), who have already thought about this, and written books about it even.
